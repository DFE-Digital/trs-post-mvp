Do you know your National Insurance number?

It's on your National Insurance card, benefit letter, payslip or P60 – for example, 'QQ 12 34 56 C'
What is a National Insurance number?

In the UK, a National Insurance number is a personal number used by HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) to record the tax and National Insurance you pay. You keep the same number for life, even if you move out of the UK.

It’s shown on UK documents such as your National Insurance card, benefit letter, payslip or P60.
